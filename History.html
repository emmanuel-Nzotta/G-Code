History of the internet (including important dates):

History in:

Message:

1969:

The first successful ARPANET (Advanced Researched Projects Agency Network) goes online on the date of October 29, 1969. The first message was sent for UCLA to Stanford University through 2 computers.

TCP - IP

1970:

In 1970 the Transmission Control Protocol and the Internet Protocol were developed between 2 computers that was running on 2 different networks, which was forming the foundation of what we call the internet. The term was discovered through the words interconnected networks.

Electronic Mail

1971:

Raymond Samuel Tomlinson was the first person who invented an email program. The program was invented in 1971. Raymond Samuel Tomlinson was an American computer programmer. The first email program was called ARPANET. The first email he sent was “something like QWERTYUIOP” which is the first row of alphabetic letters on a keyboard.

Domain Name

1985:

The first “.com” domains were created on March 15, 1985. Symbolics was the company that invented the “.com” domains. Domains refers to any users, devices, printers, or computers that can share different types of data through network resources.

WWW NOW!

1989:

he World Wide Web went live in 1989 after Tim Berners – Lee wrote 3 technologies called URL, HTML and HTTP. The first information that was posted on the web page was information on how to use the World Wide Web pages

Browser and Server

1990:

The first browser and server were created in 1990 for the World Wide Web pages.

WIFI

1997:

The first Cut the Cord Wi-Fi was invented in 1997 by Vic Hayes. He was often referring to as the Father of Wi-Fi. He was the head of the committee that invented wireless Wi-Fi.

Facebook

2004:

Facebook was launched on February 4, 2004. The inventor of Facebook is Mark Zuckerberg. He invented Facebook only for Harvard students. Not knowing that it would become a global social media platform.

Iphone Release

2007:

Apple released the first ever iPhone on June 29, 2007. The release of the iPhone transformed the way people interacted with the internet almost immediately.
